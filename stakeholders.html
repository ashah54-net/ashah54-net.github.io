Stakeholders

Recommendations for Safer ChatGPT Use

The main audience for this project is educational institutions, students, teachers, developers, healthcare organizations, employers, and policy makers. These groups should care because ChatGPT affects learning, fairness, privacy, employment, and public trust.

Students and Teachers

Need clear rules that explain when AI is allowed and when it becomes academic dishonesty.

Developers

Should design AI tools that guide users, explain limits, and encourage critical thinking.

Organizations

Need privacy policies, human review, and training before using AI in sensitive areas.

Future Recommendations

01

Teach AI Literacy

Schools and workplaces should teach users how to check AI answers, identify bias, and use AI responsibly.

02

Keep Humans in Control

AI should support decisions, but humans should remain responsible in education, healthcare, and employment.

03

Design for Learning

Developers should create tools that ask guiding questions and explain reasoning instead of only giving final answers.

04

Create Clear Policies

Institutions should explain acceptable AI use so students and workers understand expectations.

05

Protect Privacy

Users should avoid entering sensitive personal, medical, school, or workplace information into AI systems.

06

Support Regulation

Policy makers should encourage transparency, accountability, and safety standards for high-impact AI use.

One-Page Improvement Summary

Based on feedback from classmates and discussion forum comments, our team improved the final project website by strengthening both the content and presentation of our analysis on ChatGPT. One major improvement was expanding our discussion on how ChatGPT affects learning and critical thinking. A classmate raised an important question about whether instant AI-generated answers may change the way students approach problem-solving and whether developers should be concerned about this issue. In response, we added a deeper discussion about over-reliance on AI in education, explaining how students may become dependent on ChatGPT instead of building independent thinking skills. At the same time, we also emphasized that ChatGPT can support learning when used responsibly as a tutoring tool rather than a replacement for thinking.

We also improved the organization and readability of the website to better fit the needs of a general audience. Instead of relying heavily on large blocks of text, we redesigned sections to include clearer layouts, visuals, comparison tables, and structured subsections. We strengthened the ethical analysis by making clearer distinctions between utilitarianism and deontology and showing how each ethical lens interprets ChatGPT differently. Additionally, we expanded our discussion of social concerns such as misinformation, bias, privacy risks, and job displacement to better explain why the use of ChatGPT remains socially and politically complicated.

Finally, we improved the website’s visual appeal and storytelling approach to better align with the project requirements. We added clearer transitions between sections, stronger examples in education and healthcare, and more audience-friendly explanations to make the website easier to understand for non-technical users. These improvements helped us create a more engaging, informative, and balanced final project website.
