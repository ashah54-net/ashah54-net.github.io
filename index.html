ChatGPT: Helpful Tool or Threat to Human Thinking?

ChatGPT is changing how people learn, work, communicate, and make decisions. This project examines its real-world uses, social risks, and ethical questions.

Explore the Technology View Ethical Analysis

Project Focus

Our team chose ChatGPT because it is one of the most visible examples of generative AI in everyday life. Students use it for studying, workers use it for productivity, developers use it for coding, and organizations use it for communication and automation. Because it is already affecting education, healthcare, employment, and information access, ChatGPT gives us a real and current technology to analyze.

🎓

Education

ChatGPT can help students learn, but it can also weaken problem-solving if students rely on it too much.

🏥

Healthcare

AI can reduce documentation work, but mistakes in healthcare can directly affect safety and trust.

⚖️

Ethics

Using utilitarianism and deontology helps show how the same technology can be seen in different ways.

Central Question

How can society use ChatGPT to improve learning, productivity, and accessibility while reducing harm from misinformation, bias, privacy risks, overreliance, and job displacement?

1. Understand

Explain what ChatGPT is and why it matters.

2. Apply

Analyze real-world uses in education and healthcare.

3. Evaluate

Use ethical lenses to discuss social impact.

4. Recommend

Suggest safer and more responsible ways to use AI.
