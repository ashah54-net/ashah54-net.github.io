IT-Related Ethics

Ethics in information technology is important because computing professionals create systems that can affect privacy, security, fairness, and public trust.

Guidelines for Developers

  • Develop software that supports the public good.
  • Avoid harm by testing systems carefully and reducing security risks.
  • Be honest and trustworthy when designing, building, and maintaining systems.
  • Respect privacy and protect confidential user information.
  • Maintain high standards of quality, safety, and professionalism.

Ethical Considerations for Data Scientists

  • Use data responsibly and only for appropriate purposes.
  • Watch for bias in datasets and models.
  • Promote fairness, accountability, and transparency in AI systems.
  • Design systems that are reliable, secure, and understandable.
  • Consider how technology decisions affect people and society.

Why Ethics Matters in IT

IT ethics matters because computers and software are part of daily life. A poorly designed system can expose private information, create unfair outcomes, or cause harm to users. Ethical standards help developers and data professionals make responsible choices that protect people and improve trust in technology.
